Pythagoras and His Contributions

Who Was Pythagoras?

Pythagoras was an ancient Greek mathematician and philosopher born around 570 BCE on the island of Samos. He founded the Pythagorean school, which believed that numbers and mathematical relationships form the basis of the universe.

The Pythagorean Theorem

Pythagoras is best known for the Pythagorean Theorem. It states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. This theorem is widely used in construction, engineering, navigation, and modern technology.

Contributions to Mathematics

Pythagoras and his followers contributed to early number theory by studying numerical patterns, even and odd numbers, and mathematical relationships. Their work helped shape logical and mathematical thinking.

Music and Science

Pythagoras discovered that musical harmony is based on simple numerical ratios. This showed a strong connection between mathematics and music, linking numbers with sound and rhythm.
