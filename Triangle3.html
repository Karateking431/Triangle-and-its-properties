Pythagoras and His Contributions

Who Was Pythagoras?

Pythagoras was an ancient Greek mathematician and philosopher born around 570 BCE on the island of Samos. He founded the Pythagorean school, which believed that numbers and mathematical relationships form the foundation of the universe.

The Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. It is widely used in construction, engineering, and navigation.

Contributions to Mathematics

Pythagoras and his followers studied numerical patterns, even and odd numbers, and mathematical relationships. Their work helped establish early number theory and logical reasoning.

Music and Legacy

Pythagoras discovered that musical harmony follows simple numerical ratios, linking mathematics with music. His ideas influenced future thinkers such as Plato and remain important in modern education and science.
