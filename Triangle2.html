Angle Sum Property of a Triangle

Statement:
The sum of the three interior angles of a triangle is always 180°.

Explanation:
No matter how big or small the triangle is, if you add all its interior angles, the total will always be 180°. This property helps us find an unknown angle when the other two angles are known.

Example:
If two angles are 50° and 60°, then Third angle = 180° − (50° + 60°) = 70°

Triangle Inequality Property

Statement:
The sum of any two sides of a triangle is greater than the third side.

Explanation:
This property tells us whether three given lengths can actually form a triangle or not. If the sum of two sides is equal to or less than the third side, a triangle cannot be formed.

Example:
Sides: 4 cm, 5 cm, 8 cm 4 + 5 = 9 > 8 ✅ So, a triangle can be formed. But sides: 3 cm, 4 cm, 7 cm 3 + 4 = 7 ❌ So, a triangle cannot be formed.
